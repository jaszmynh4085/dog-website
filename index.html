Breeds:


339 breeds of dogs which are divided into groups based upon the dog’s purpose or function.


Herding


Examples:
German shepherds
large dog (females: 50-70 pounds, 22-24 inches, 65-90 pounds, 24-26 inches) Life expectancy of 7-10 years

Sheltie (The Shetland Sheepdog)
Small dog (15-25 pounds, 13-16 inches) Life expectancy of 12-14 years

Bearded Collie (Beardie)
Medium to Large dog (45-55 pounds, female: 20-21 inches, male:21-22 inches) Life expectancy of 12-14 years



Working


examples:
German pinscher
Medium dog (25-45 pounds, 17,20 inches) Life expectancy of 12-14 years

boxer
Large dog (male: 65-80 pounds, 23-25 inches, female: 15 pounds less than male, 21.5-23.5 inches) Life expectancy of 10-12 years

boerboel
Ex Large dog (150-200 pounds, male: 24-27 inches, female: 22-25 inches) Life expectancy of 9-11 years



Toy group


examples:
silky terrier Miniature dog (Around 10 pounds, 9-10 inches) Life expectancy of 13-15 years

chihuahua
Miniature dog (at most 6 pounds, 5-8 inches) Life expectancy of 14-16 years

maltese
Miniature dog (under 7 pounds, 7-9 inches) Life expectancy of 12-15 years



Terrier


Examples:
Manchester terrier
Small dog (12-22 pounds, 15-16 inches) Life expectancy of 15-17 years

Australian terrier
Small dog (15-20 pounds, 10-11 inches) Life expectancy of 11-15 years

border terrier
Small dog (female: 11.5-14 pounds, male: 13-15.5 pounds, 12-15 inches) Life expectancy of 12-15 years



Sporting


Examples:
American water spaniel
Small to Medium dog (male: 30-45 pounds, female: 25-40 pounds, 15-18 inches) Life expectancy of 10-14 years

cocker spaniel
Small dog (male: 25-30 pounds, 14.5-15.5 inches, female: 20-25 pounds, 13.5-14.5 inches) Life expectancy of 10-14 years

flat coated retriever
Large dog (60-70 pounds, male: 23-24.5 inches, female: 22-23.5 inches) Life expectancy of 8-10 years



Non-sporting


examples:
bulldog
Medium dog (male: 50 pounds, Female: 40 pounds, 14-15 inches) Life expectancy of 8-10 years

American Eskimo dog
Small dog (standard 23-25 pounds, 15-19 inches) (can be smaller) Life expectancy of 13-15 years

dalmatian
Medium to large dog (45-70 pounds, 19-24 inches) Life expectancy of 11-13 years



Hound


Examples:
bloodhound
Large dog (90-110 pounds, male: 25-27 inches, female: 23-25 inches) Life expectancy of 10-12 years

basset hound
Medium to large dog (40-65 pounds, up to 15 inches) Life expectancy of 12-13 years

beagle
Small dog (under 20 pounds when 13 inches and under, 20-30 pounds when 13-15 inches) Life expectancy of 10-15 years

More on sizes:


*All sizes depends on who you ask.*
*Some places have medium/large dogs starting at 55 pounds others at 50 pounds and small/medium at 25-35 pounds*

For a dog to be considered a large dog breed they have to weigh at least 50 pounds and have a height of (around) 24 inches. From there breeders separate the dogs further. Dogs over 100 pounds will be considered giant dogs. Officially any dog that weighs more than 50 pounds but less than 100 are large dogs


Small to Medium sized dogs are about 18-22 inches in height and weigh about 35-65 (or 40-60) pounds


Small dog breeds may be around 10 inches. Then there are miniature dog breeds such as toy dogs that vary between 4 pounds and 12 pounds and teacup dogs that are smaller than usual toy dogs
